# Verification Framework

Verification is a core component of LazosTech.

The platform ensures that activities proposed through governance processes are **actually executed and validated before rewards are distributed**.

This document describes how the verification system works and how it helps prevent fraud.

---

# Why Verification Matters

Incentive systems can be vulnerable to abuse if actions are not verified.

Without verification mechanisms, participants could submit false activity records in order to obtain rewards.

LazosTech introduces verification layers to ensure that:

• submitted activities are legitimate
• outcomes match the governance resolution
• incentives are distributed fairly

---

# Verification Lifecycle

The verification process occurs after an activity has been executed.

```
Activity Submission
        ↓
Initial Record Validation
        ↓
Verifier Review
        ↓
Verification Approval
        ↓
Reward Distribution
```

Only activities that successfully pass verification become eligible for incentives.

---

# Activity Submission

Participants submit activity records to the system.

Submissions may include:

• description of the activity
• timestamp
• location or context
• supporting documentation

The submission becomes a **verifiable on-chain record**.

---

# Verification Roles

Verification can be performed by different types of actors depending on the governance structure.

Possible verifiers include:

• designated institutional verifiers
• governance participants
• trusted community members
• automated verification systems

The governance structure determines who is authorized to verify specific types of activities.

---

# Multi-Party Verification

To increase reliability, the system may require **multiple independent verifiers**.

This approach reduces the risk of fraudulent approvals.

Examples include:

• two verifiers approving a recycling submission
• institutional validation combined with community confirmation
• multi-signature verification

Multi-party verification strengthens trust in the system.

---

# Recycling Verification

For recycling workflows, verification may include confirmation that recyclable materials were properly delivered or processed.

Possible verification approaches include:

• confirmation by recycling centers
• verification by institutional staff
• photographic or digital evidence
• community validation

Only verified recycling actions can generate token rewards.

---

# Anti-Fraud Mechanisms

The platform can implement several mechanisms to prevent abuse.

These may include:

• verifier reputation systems
• multi-party verification requirements
• limits on reward claims
• anomaly detection for suspicious activity patterns

These mechanisms help maintain **integrity within the incentive system**.

---

# Governance Control

Governance can adjust verification parameters, including:

• number of required verifiers
• eligibility of verifiers
• verification time windows
• reward thresholds

This flexibility allows institutions to adapt verification rules to their specific needs.

---

# Transparency

All verification decisions are recorded on-chain.

This ensures that:

• verification actions are auditable
• reward distributions can be traced
• disputes can be reviewed transparently

Transparency strengthens trust in the governance and incentive mechanisms.

---

# Future Verification Improvements

Future development may include:

• reputation-based verification systems
• automated validation using external data sources
• integration with IoT or sensor-based tracking
• decentralized oracle verification

These improvements will further enhance the reliability and scalability of the verification framework.

---

# Summary

Verification ensures that governance decisions lead to **real-world actions and measurable results**.

By combining structured verification workflows with transparent records and incentive alignment, LazosTech provides a reliable foundation for **impact-driven governance systems**.
