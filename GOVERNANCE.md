# Governance Model

LazosTech implements a **multi-level governance structure** designed to support institutional decision-making while maintaining protocol-level stability.

The governance model separates **strategic governance from operational execution**.

---

# Governance Layers

The platform includes two primary governance layers:

• Board Governance
• Institutional Governance

---

# Board Governance

Board Governance acts as the **highest authority of the protocol**.

Responsibilities include:

• protocol upgrades
• economic parameters
• reward distribution rules
• governance policies
• system-level configuration

The board ensures the **long-term stability and sustainability of the platform**.

---

# Institutional Governance

Institutional governance enables organizations such as universities to manage operational initiatives.

Responsibilities include:

• proposing activities
• coordinating projects
• executing initiatives
• verifying outcomes
• managing participation

Institutional governance cannot modify **protocol-level parameters** without board approval.

---

# Proposal Lifecycle

Governance proposals follow a structured lifecycle.

```
Proposal Creation
        ↓
Deliberation
        ↓
Voting
        ↓
Resolution
        ↓
Execution
        ↓
Verification
```

This structure ensures that governance decisions are **traceable and verifiable**.

---

# Voting Mechanisms

Voting mechanisms may include:

• token-based voting
• delegated voting
• institutional voting rights

Voting models can be adapted depending on the **governance context**.

---

# Execution Layer

Once a proposal is approved, the system enables:

• assignment of responsible participants
• activity execution tracking
• verification of outcomes

Execution ensures governance decisions result in **real-world action**.

---

# Verification

Verification ensures that executed activities meet the conditions defined in governance resolutions.

Verification may include:

• designated verifiers
• institutional validation
• multi-party confirmation

Verification enables the **incentive layer** to distribute rewards.

---

# Incentive Alignment

Participants who execute verified activities can receive **NUDOS token rewards**.

This creates an incentive model aligned with:

• governance participation
• environmental contributions
• social impact activities

---

# Upgrade Governance

Because LazosTech uses the **Diamond Standard (EIP-2535)**, upgrades occur through governance decisions.

Upgrades may include:

• new facets
• updated governance rules
• new impact modules

This architecture allows the protocol to evolve while maintaining continuity.

---

# Governance Principles

The governance system is designed around the following principles:

• transparency
• accountability
• verifiable execution
• modular evolution
• incentive alignment
