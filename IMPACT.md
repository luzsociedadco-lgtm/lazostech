# Impact Framework

LazosTech is designed to connect governance decisions with **measurable social and environmental impact**.

The platform provides infrastructure for organizations to propose initiatives, execute activities, verify results, and distribute incentives based on verified outcomes.

This document describes how LazosTech tracks and evaluates impact within the ecosystem.

---

# Impact Philosophy

The platform is built around the principle that **governance should produce verifiable results**.

Traditional governance systems often record decisions but do not ensure that actions are executed or that results are measured.

LazosTech introduces a framework where:

• decisions are recorded on-chain
• activities are tracked during execution
• outcomes are verified
• incentives are distributed based on verified impact

This creates a transparent cycle connecting **decision-making and real-world outcomes**.

---

# Impact Lifecycle

Impact in LazosTech follows a structured lifecycle.

```
Governance Decision
        ↓
Activity Execution
        ↓
Outcome Verification
        ↓
Impact Recognition
        ↓
Incentive Distribution
```

Each stage ensures that actions are not only proposed but also **completed and validated**.

---

# Environmental Impact

One of the primary focus areas of LazosTech is **environmental sustainability**, particularly recycling initiatives.

Institutions and communities can organize recycling campaigns where participants submit records of recycling contributions.

These contributions are verified before rewards are issued.

Examples of measurable environmental contributions include:

• recycling materials
• waste reduction campaigns
• sustainability education programs
• campus environmental initiatives

---

# Social Impact

Beyond environmental programs, the platform supports broader social initiatives.

Organizations can use LazosTech to coordinate and verify activities such as:

• community projects
• volunteer programs
• educational initiatives
• social innovation projects

By recording participation and verifying completion, the system provides **transparent accountability for social programs**.

# Institutional Impact

Institutions such as universities can use LazosTech to:

• manage governance processes
• track implementation of decisions
• coordinate environmental initiatives
• measure participation in institutional programs

This enables institutions to demonstrate **accountability and measurable outcomes**.

---

# Impact Metrics

Impact can be measured using multiple indicators depending on the initiative.

Examples of impact metrics include:

Environmental metrics

• kilograms of recycled materials
• number of recycling actions
• participation in environmental campaigns

Participation metrics

• number of contributors
• governance participation rate
• volunteer engagement

Execution metrics

• activities completed
• verified initiatives
• successful governance resolutions executed

These metrics help organizations understand **the real outcomes of governance decisions**.

---

# Transparency

All governance decisions, activity execution records, and reward distributions are recorded on-chain.

This enables:

• public transparency
• independent verification
• long-term accountability

Organizations can use these records to demonstrate impact to stakeholders, partners, and communities.

---

# Incentives and Impact

The NUDOS token aligns incentives with positive contributions.

Participants can receive rewards for:

• executing approved initiatives
• contributing to environmental programs
• supporting governance processes

By connecting incentives with verified contributions, the platform encourages **sustained engagement in social and environmental initiatives**.

---

# Long-Term Vision

LazosTech aims to become infrastructure for organizations that want to:

• coordinate governance transparently
• measure real-world impact
• incentivize participation in sustainability initiatives

By combining governance systems with environmental and social impact tracking, the platform supports a future where **collective decisions produce measurable and verifiable results**.
